My internship at Fortress E&P Ghana (October–December 2024) was a highly rewarding experience that allowed me to develop practical skills in web development, robotics, mechanical design, and teamwork through diverse and challenging projects. Despite initial challenges due to the absence of key personnel, the supportive workplace environment and collaborative efforts of the team ensured I could overcome these hurdles and contribute meaningfully to the projects.

Read the full project page here: [Fortress Visitor Log System project page](/eadwine/docs/internship-projects/visitor-log).

### Internship Overview

This industrial attachment was highly relevant to my studies in Mechatronics Engineering and provided valuable practical exposure to robotics and systems programming. The experience bridged academic knowledge with real industry practice, significantly enhancing my technical abilities, problem-solving skills, and confidence in handling multidisciplinary engineering tasks.

### Key Projects and Contributions

**Visitor Log System Development**
- Assisted in the development of a Visitor Log System backend using Python for Fortress Ghana.
- Contributed to frontend and backend workflow implementation for visitor entry control and record keeping.
- Documented research and development work in a formal report using Microsoft Word.

**ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle) Work**
- Assisted with the testing of ROV equipment and machinery for offshore operations.
- Participated in the calibration of DC Motors for ROV development and research.
- Tested ROV sensors connected to a server using fiber-optic cables.

**Mechanical & Design Work**
- Assisted in the design of a mini-drone using Fusion 360 for demonstration and R&D purposes.
- Contributed to the design of a mechanical mount for a printed circuit board in an ROV compartment.

### Technologies and Tools Used

- **Programming**: Python for automation and support scripts, backend system development.
- **Embedded Systems**: Segger Embedded Studio, hardware debugging via Segger J-Link, STM32 microcontrollers.
- **CAD & Mechanical Design**: Fusion 360 for drone and ROV mount design.
- **Circuit Design**: Basic circuit design and component integration for charger modules and test electronics.
- **Hardware Testing**: Sensor calibration, motor testing, and system integration validation.

### What I Learned

- **Systems Integration**: How to apply embedded systems knowledge to real workplace problems and coordinate solutions across multiple disciplines.
- **Problem-Solving Under Constraints**: Developing creative solutions when key personnel were unavailable, learning to be resourceful and adaptable.
- **Professional Development**: Working in an industrial environment taught me the importance of documentation, systematic testing, and collaborative engineering.
- **Hands-On Robotics**: Practical experience with ROV systems, sensor integration, and motor control in a production context.
- **Multidisciplinary Teamwork**: How to bridge firmware, mechanical design, and systems engineering to deliver integrated solutions.

### Internship Impact

This experience bridged the gap between academic knowledge and industry practice. It reinforced the value of mechatronics engineering as a multidisciplinary field and demonstrated how embedded systems, robotics, and mechanical design converge in real-world applications. The internship strengthened my confidence in applying theoretical concepts to practical engineering challenges and highlighted the importance of clear communication, documentation, and systematic problem-solving in professional settings.

The Fortress internship remains a defining experience in my engineering journey, shaping how I approach complex, multidisciplinary projects and team collaboration.
